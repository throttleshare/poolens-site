DE Filter Charge Guide: How Much DE to Add and When

📅 September 22, 2025Equipment⏱ 6 min read

Diatomaceous earth filters are the most powerful filtration option in the residential pool market — but only when properly charged. Too little DE and water bypasses the grids unfiltered. Too much and you get premature pressure rise and restricted flow. This guide covers exact amounts by filter model, when to recharge versus deep-clean, and how to add DE cleanly.

The Rule: 1 Pound Per 10 Square Feet

The universal starting point for DE dosing is 1 lb of DE powder per 10 sq ft of filter surface area. After a standard backwash, you add back roughly 80% of the full charge (since backwashing is never 100% efficient — some DE always remains on the grids).

When to Recharge vs. When to Deep Clean

Standard Recharge (After Backwash)

Every time you backwash, you must add DE back. Backwashing removes 20–30% of the DE along with the debris. Without recharging, the grids are partially exposed and water passes through unfiltered — you'll see it in the water clarity.

Annual Deep Clean (Disassembly)

Once per season (or when pressure rises and backwashing no longer brings it down), the filter requires full disassembly and manual grid cleaning. Remove the manifold, pull each grid, and hose them off carefully. Inspect grids for tears, collapsed spines, or calcification. Replace any damaged grids — a single torn grid means unfiltered water returning to the pool.

DE that has been in service through a heavy algae bloom or multiple chemical treatments can develop calcified deposits that hosing won't remove. Soak grids in a 10:1 water-to-muriatic acid solution for 15 minutes, then rinse thoroughly before reinstalling.

How to Add DE Powder Without Making a Mess

  1. After backwash, move valve to Filter and start the pump. The grids inside are now clean (or mostly clean) and ready to receive the new DE coat.
  2. Measure DE into a bucket. Add enough water to make a slurry — roughly 1 gallon of water per pound of DE.
  3. Walk to the pool skimmer nearest the filter return.
  4. Pour the DE slurry slowly into the skimmer while the pump is running. The suction draws it directly to the filter, coating the grids evenly.
  5. Rinse the bucket into the skimmer to capture any remaining powder.
  6. Wait 5–10 minutes before checking the pressure gauge — it takes a few minutes for the DE to fully coat and seat on the grids.

Never dump dry DE powder directly into the skimmer. It puffs up and can partially escape the skimmer basket, causing a brief cloudy return. Always make a slurry first.

Signs of Improper DE Charging

  • DE returning to pool through returns: Grid is torn or manifold O-ring has failed. Inspect grids and O-rings immediately.
  • Pressure climbing to backwash point within hours of recharging: Either too much DE was added, or the grids have calcified debris that a standard backwash can't remove — time for a deep clean.
  • Dull water despite good chemistry: Undercharged filter — grids aren't coated. Add additional DE and recheck.
  • DE in bottom of pool after recharge: Minor amounts are normal; significant amounts indicate a return line flow issue or an overcharge.

DE Disposal and Environmental Considerations

Used DE can typically be disposed of in regular trash (check local codes). Do not dump backwash water containing DE into storm drains — in many states this is a code violation. Route backwash to a sanitary sewer clean-out or a dedicated drainage area on the property. Never allow DE-laden backwash to reach waterways.

If DE disposal is restricted in your service area, perlite-based alternatives like Celite 545 or commercial "DE Alternative" products provide similar filtration performance and are generally accepted for standard drain disposal.

Frequently Asked Questions

How much DE do I add after backwashing?

After a standard backwash, add back about 80% of the original full charge — roughly 1 lb of DE per 10 sq ft of filter area. Full amounts depend on your specific filter model.

Can I add too much DE to my filter?

Yes. Over-charging causes the DE to cake and restrict flow, raising pressure prematurely. Never exceed the manufacturer's recommended charge by more than 10%.

Is DE powder dangerous?

Food-grade or pool-grade DE is safe when wet, but the dry powder is a nuisance dust. Wear an N95 mask when handling dry DE to avoid inhaling the fine silica particles.

What can I use instead of DE powder?

Perlite (sold as Celite or DE Alternative) is a safer substitute with similar filtration performance. It lacks the crystalline silica found in mined DE and is easier to dispose of in many jurisdictions.
