Smart Pool Technology 2026: Automation, Apps, and AI

📅 March 10, 2026Pool Lifestyle⏱ 7 min read

The connected pool market has grown significantly. Automation systems, smart chemistry monitors, AI-driven dosing controllers, and app-based service platforms have changed how pools are managed. For pool service professionals, this technology is both an opportunity and a challenge — it creates upsell potential but also clients who adjust settings between visits and call you when their app shows an alarm.

Pool Automation Systems

A full pool automation system controls all major equipment from a central panel and smartphone app — pump speed scheduling, heater setpoints, lights, cleaner operation, water feature valves, and chemical dosing. The three dominant platforms in 2026:

Automation is most valuable when clients use their pools regularly and have heaters, color lights, water features, or other equipment they want to control without going to a panel. The ROI is strongest when combined with a variable speed pump — automation scheduling the pump at optimal speeds for filtration cycles produces the energy savings that justify part of the system cost.

Smart Chemistry Monitoring

Continuous chemistry monitoring systems measure water parameters 24/7 using probes and sensors, then report readings to an app. Systems range from simple consumer products to professional chemical automation controllers:

pHin Smart Monitor

A consumer-grade floating sensor that measures pH, ORP, and temperature every 15 minutes. Pairs with an app that recommends chemical additions and tracks water balance. Cost is around $200 for the sensor plus a $100/year subscription for chemical analysis. Good for homeowners who want visibility between service visits but shouldn't replace professional service or lab-quality testing for critical decisions.

Pentair IntelliChem

A professional-grade automated chemical dosing system that continuously monitors pH and ORP and automatically doses acid (pH down) and chlorine. Integrated with IntelliTouch automation. The IntelliChem requires probe calibration every 1–3 months and acid/chlorine solution containers to be refilled. Cost is $800–$1,200 for the unit plus installation. For high-end residential pools or commercial applications, it's a genuine automation tool.

Hayward HCC

Hayward's Halo Chemical Controller integrates with OmniLogic and uses probe-based monitoring with automated chlorine dosing via liquid chlorine feeder. Similar functionality and price point to IntelliChem but within the Hayward ecosystem.

Automated dosing systems reduce manual chemistry management — but they don't eliminate the need for manual testing and professional service. Probes drift and must be calibrated. Cyanuric acid, calcium hardness, and phosphate levels aren't measured by ORP/pH probes. Service visits remain essential even on fully automated pools. For pool service professionals, automated pools that are well-maintained are easier service stops — but use SplashLens to log your independent test readings alongside the automation data.

AI-Powered Pool Management: What's Actually Real

Several companies market "AI" pool management. In 2026, this mostly means algorithmic dosing recommendations based on historical water data and pattern recognition — not true machine learning in the AI researcher's sense. Systems like Orenda's OrendaApp use chemistry algorithms to recommend doses based on target LSI values. This is genuinely useful chemistry guidance, even if "AI" overstates the technology involved.

True AI in pool management will eventually include computer vision for detecting algae, debris, and equipment wear from cameras. Several startups are working on this for commercial pool applications. For residential pools, we're 3–5 years from practical AI-driven visual inspection tools.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a pool automation system?

A pool automation system is a control hub that manages pool equipment — pump speed, heater, lights, cleaner, valves — from a central controller and smartphone app. Major brands include Pentair IntelliTouch/IntelliConnect, Hayward OmniLogic, and Jandy iAquaLink. Systems range from $1,500 to $5,000+ installed.

Can a smart pool system automatically add chemicals?

Yes. Automatic chemical dosing systems like Hayward HCC (pH/chlorine), Pentair IntelliChem, and standalone units like Orenda's dosing controllers continuously monitor and dose pH and ORP/chlorine based on real-time probe readings. These systems reduce manual chemical management significantly but require probe calibration and maintenance.

What is ORP in pool chemistry monitoring?

ORP (Oxidation-Reduction Potential) measured in millivolts is a proxy for chlorine's effectiveness — it measures the sanitizing power of the water rather than just chlorine concentration. Most automation systems use ORP probes alongside pH probes for automated dosing control.

Is pool automation worth the cost?

For a homeowner who uses their pool regularly and wants to minimize manual management, full automation pays back through energy savings (variable speed pump scheduling), chemical savings (automated dosing reduces over-treatment), and convenience. For service companies, automation complicates servicing if the client adjusts settings between visits.

What pool apps work without an automation system?

Apps like SplashLens work without any connected equipment — they function as digital logbooks for chemistry readings, service notes, and history. Other apps like Pool Math help calculate chemical doses. These tools are useful without any investment in connected pool hardware.
