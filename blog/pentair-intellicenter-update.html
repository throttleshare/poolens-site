Pentair IntelliCenter 2026 Update: New Features for Pool Pros

📅 December 7, 2025Industry News⏱ 6 min read

Pentair's IntelliCenter platform — the successor to EasyTouch and IntelliTouch — has become the automation system of record for new pool installations in most major markets. Understanding the current feature set and 2025–2026 updates helps pool service techs troubleshoot more effectively, upsell upgrades confidently, and serve the growing installed base of IntelliCenter customers.

What IntelliCenter Does (The Foundation)

IntelliCenter is a full pool and spa automation controller. At its core, it manages:

  • Variable speed pump speed scheduling (up to 8 programs per pump)
  • Heater/heat pump control with temperature setpoints and schedules
  • Salt system (IntelliChlor) management with chlorine output percentage
  • Lights (IntelliBrite LED and standard) with color and scene control
  • Auxiliary circuits (waterfall, cleaner pump, deck jets, spa jets)
  • Chemical controller integration (IntelliChem for pH and ORP dosing)

All of this is controlled via the touchscreen control panel, the ScreenLogic2 mobile app, Amazon Alexa, Google Home, and Apple HomeKit.

2025–2026 Updates

ScreenLogic2 App Overhaul

The most significant update is to the ScreenLogic2 mobile app. The redesigned interface is cleaner, with a new dashboard view showing all active equipment status at a glance. Push notifications have been improved — the system can now send alerts for high/low water temperature (freeze protection), equipment faults, and salt cell status changes. For techs who help customers set up app monitoring, this makes the customer experience significantly better.

IntelliChem Integration Updates

IntelliCenter's integration with Pentair's IntelliChem chemical controller has been tightened. IntelliChem measures ORP (a proxy for sanitizer effectiveness) and pH, and automatically doses acid and chlorine to maintain setpoints. The 2025 firmware update improved the dosing algorithm's response to rapid chemistry changes (heavy bather load, rain dilution events) and added data logging accessible through the IntelliCenter history screen.

IntelliChem is a genuine automation upgrade for commercial and high-use residential pools. For residential pools serviced weekly by a competent tech, it's an optional convenience. The question to ask: does this customer's lifestyle justify automated dosing, or do weekly service visits provide sufficient control?

Freeze Protection Improvements

The 2025 firmware update improved freeze protection logic for markets with occasional freeze events (Florida, Texas, Arizona). When outdoor temperature drops below a configurable threshold, IntelliCenter now automatically sequences the pump on, opens specific valves, and sends app alerts — even if the system was in manual off mode. For pool service companies managing properties in freeze-prone areas, this reduces emergency calls significantly.

Energy Management Reporting

A new energy reporting feature in the dashboard tracks estimated kWh consumption by equipment over time. This is primarily useful for demonstrating the ROI of variable speed pump upgrades to customers — showing actual energy consumption data rather than hypothetical savings estimates.

For Pool Service Techs: The Practical Guide

Getting Into IntelliCenter for Service

Most IntelliCenter systems are accessible through three paths:

  1. Touchscreen panel at the equipment pad: No credentials needed for manual control. The service mode puts the system under direct manual control.
  2. ScreenLogic2 app (with customer WiFi credentials): Remote access for programming, scheduling, and diagnostics without being at the equipment pad.
  3. Local network connection: Connect directly to the IntelliCenter on the local network for firmware updates and advanced diagnostics.

Common Service Issues

  • IntelliChlor "No Flow" error: Most commonly caused by a clogged filter, closed valve, or failed flow switch. Check filter pressure first.
  • Pump losing prime after power cycle: Check the pump strainer lid O-ring and ensure the lid is fully seated.
  • App connection loss: Usually a router/WiFi issue, not IntelliCenter. The system requires a 2.4 GHz network; 5 GHz-only routers are a common issue.
  • Heater fault codes: Reference the Pentair service documentation for specific codes. Most common: E05 (pressure switch), E06 (flow switch), E10 (high limit).

Upgrade Path from EasyTouch

Many pools installed between 2010–2018 have Pentair EasyTouch systems. EasyTouch does not have app connectivity and is approaching end-of-life for parts support. Upgrading these systems to IntelliCenter is one of the most natural upsells for a pool tech servicing Pentair equipment — total cost typically $2,500–$4,500 installed, and the customer experience improvement (app control, scheduling, notifications) is immediately tangible.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is Pentair IntelliCenter?

IntelliCenter is Pentair's pool automation and control system. It manages pump speed, heater, lights, cleaner, salt system, and chemical feed via a touchscreen panel and smartphone app, replacing the older EasyTouch and IntelliTouch systems.

How does IntelliCenter differ from IntelliConnect?

IntelliConnect is a simpler connectivity add-on for existing VS pumps — it controls pump speed only. IntelliCenter is a full automation system managing all pool equipment.

Can Pentair IntelliCenter integrate with smart home systems?

Yes. IntelliCenter supports Amazon Alexa, Google Home, and Apple HomeKit. It also has an open API for integration with Crestron, Control4, and other home automation systems.

How do pool techs access IntelliCenter for diagnostics?

Via the touchscreen panel, the ScreenLogic2 app (with the customer's WiFi credentials), or a direct network connection. Pentair's Pro program provides access to diagnostic tools and firmware update management.

What does IntelliCenter cost installed?

IntelliCenter systems range from $1,800–$3,500 for equipment alone, with installation adding $500–$1,500. Upgrading from EasyTouch to IntelliCenter typically costs $2,500–$4,500 total.
