Pentair SuperFlo VS: Best Budget Variable Speed Pump?

📅 March 18, 2026Products⏱ 6 min read

The Pentair IntelliFlo gets all the attention, but the SuperFlo VS is Pentair's more affordable variable speed pump that delivers most of the IntelliFlo's energy savings at a significantly lower price point. For pool service professionals installing VSPs on budget-conscious accounts, the SuperFlo VS deserves serious consideration.

What Makes It "Budget"

The SuperFlo VS has 4 programmable speed settings compared to the IntelliFlo's 8. It uses RPM-based control rather than GPM (flow) feedback — meaning it maintains a target speed, not a target flow. It has a smaller strainer basket capacity than the IntelliFlo. These limitations matter primarily for complex pool systems with variable head pressure, spas, or multiple circulation circuits. For a standard single-body residential pool, the practical impact of these differences is minimal.

Energy Savings: How Close to the IntelliFlo?

The permanent magnet motor in the SuperFlo VS is the same technology as the IntelliFlo. At equivalent speeds, the energy consumption is nearly identical. The energy savings come from running at low speeds — and the SuperFlo VS runs at the same low speeds as the IntelliFlo. Annual energy savings of $300–$500 are achievable with the SuperFlo VS on a standard 20,000-gallon residential pool, essentially the same as its premium sibling.

Who the SuperFlo VS Is Perfect For

The SuperFlo VS is the ideal replacement pump for:

  • Standard residential inground pools without spas or complex multi-circuit systems
  • Pools where the client wants energy savings but not the premium price of IntelliFlo
  • Pool replacement situations where existing plumbing limits flow requirements
  • Pools running Pentair EasyTouch (lower-end Pentair automation) rather than IntelliTouch

Installation Notes for Service Pros

The SuperFlo VS has a slightly different footprint than older SuperFlo single-speed pumps. Check the pump footprint and union connection orientation before ordering — some installations require brief plumbing modifications. The 4-speed program format is simpler to configure than the IntelliFlo's 8-program setup, which can be an advantage for clients who want to understand and interact with their pump schedule.

Program the SuperFlo VS with overnight low-speed filtration (1,000–1,200 RPM), mid-speed daytime filtration (1,800–2,200 RPM), and high-speed peak use periods (2,500–3,000 RPM). For salt pools, ensure the minimum speed program during salt cell operating hours meets the cell's minimum flow requirement (typically 1,500–2,000 RPM for most residential cells).

When clients ask which pump to buy, the recommendation is straightforward: if they have a simple residential pool without a spa or complex multi-zone system, the SuperFlo VS delivers 90%+ of the IntelliFlo's energy savings at 70–75% of the cost. SuperFlo VS vs Hayward TriStar VS

These two pumps compete directly. Both are similarly priced ($500–$700), both use permanent magnet VSP motors, both have Pentair and Hayward automation integration respectively. The practical difference for most residential installations is negligible — match the pump brand to the existing automation system. Pentair pool = SuperFlo VS. Hayward pool = TriStar VS. Mixed system = either works with the appropriate RS-485 adapter.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between Pentair SuperFlo VS and IntelliFlo?

The SuperFlo VS maintains a target RPM; the IntelliFlo maintains a target GPM (flow rate). The IntelliFlo's flow feedback automatically adjusts to compensate for filter loading and head pressure changes. The SuperFlo VS is more affordable and performs well for most residential pools where precise flow control isn't critical.

How much does the Pentair SuperFlo VS cost?

The SuperFlo VS retails for $500–$650 depending on horsepower and retailer. Installed cost with professional labor runs $700–$1,100. It's Pentair's most affordable variable speed pump for inground pools.

Does the SuperFlo VS integrate with Pentair automation?

The SuperFlo VS integrates with Pentair EasyTouch and IntelliTouch automation systems via RS-485 communication. This allows speed scheduling and remote monitoring from the ScreenLogic app. It does not integrate with non-Pentair automation systems without a third-party adapter.

How long does the Pentair SuperFlo VS last?

Pentair variable speed pumps typically last 10–14 years with normal residential use. The permanent magnet motor in the SuperFlo VS is simpler than traditional induction motors and generally has fewer failure modes. Pentair backs the motor with a 2-year warranty.

Can I install the Pentair SuperFlo VS myself?

In some jurisdictions, homeowner installation of pool pumps is allowed. However, the SuperFlo VS requires 240V electrical connection, which should be handled by a licensed electrician. Plumbing connections are straightforward for someone comfortable with PVC work. Check local codes before DIY installation.
