## Module Outline

### MODULE 1: Pool Chemistry Fundamentals (Days 1–3)
**Learning outcome:** Tech can read any test result and know what it means before touching a chemical.

Topics:
- The LSI (Langelier Saturation Index) — what it is, why it matters, how to eyeball it
- The 5 numbers that matter: FC, pH, TA, CH, CYA
- How FC and CYA interact — why the same FC reading is fine in one pool and dangerous in another
- Dead zones: pH <7.2, CYA >100, FC <breakpoint — what happens in each
- How to read a Taylor K-2006 test kit (most common kit in the industry)
- How to read a photometer / liquid test strips / digital meter
- When to trust a test vs. when to re-test

**SplashLens tie-in:** Chemical Dosing tab — walk through each calculator live  
**Assessment:** Given these test results, what's wrong and what do you dose first? (3 scenarios)

---

### MODULE 2: Dosing Math Without a Calculator (Days 3–5)
**Learning outcome:** Tech can estimate a dose in their head and use the app to confirm it.

Topics:
- Pool volume estimation — rectangle, circle, kidney, L-shape; the shortcut formulas
- Chemical strength basics (trichlor 90%, dichlor 56%, cal-hypo 65%, liquid 10–12.5%)
- "Per 10,000 gallon" rules of thumb for each chemical
- Mixing sequence — what order to add chemicals, why it matters, what never to do
- Dangerous combinations: chlorine + acid, different sanitizers, cal-hypo + trichlor

**SplashLens tie-in:** Pool Volume Calculator + Chemical Safety Guide  
**Assessment:** Pool is 22,000 gallons. FC is 1.0, CYA is 50. Dose to 5 ppm FC using liquid chlorine (10%). How many ounces?

---

### MODULE 3: Equipment 101 — Pumps, Filters, Heaters (Days 5–8)
**Learning outcome:** Tech can identify major components, explain how they work, and spot obvious failures.

Topics:
- The circulation loop: pump → filter → heater → sanitizer → back to pool
- Single-speed vs. variable speed pumps — why VSP matters for customer energy bills
- Filter types: sand (backwash), DE (re-charge + tear-down), cartridge (clean or replace)
- Heater types: gas, heat pump, solar — how each works, what each error code category means
- Salt chlorine generators: how they work, target salt levels, cell cleaning, low-flow errors
- Pressure gauge reading — high vs. low pressure, what each means

**SplashLens tie-in:** Error Code Database + Filter Guides  
**Assessment:** Filter pressure is 25 psi. Normal starting pressure is 12 psi. What are the two most likely causes and what do you do first?

---

### MODULE 4: Reading Error Codes in the Field (Days 8–10)
**Learning outcome:** Tech can look up any error code they encounter and diagnose the root cause, not just reset the fault.

Topics:
- Why error codes are diagnoses, not problems — the E-code is a symptom
- The four most common categories: flow errors, ignition errors, sensor errors, communication errors
- Reading a Hayward display vs. Pentair vs. Jandy — they don't use the same system
- When to reset vs. when to investigate vs. when to escalate
- Documentation: how to log an error code so the next tech knows what you found

**SplashLens tie-in:** Error Code Database — AI Scanner demo  
**Real case studies:** 10 error codes with diagnosis walkthrough (FLO, E05, EE, SVC, HLS, −−−, ComError, SPK, IGN, LO)

---

### MODULE 5: Running a Weekly Service Route (Days 10–14)
**Learning outcome:** Tech can independently complete a weekly service call from arrival to documentation, every time.

Topics:
- The 12-step weekly checklist — in the right order for efficiency and safety
- What to look for before you open the equipment pad
- Water balance vs. "dump and shock" — why the shotgun approach burns customers
- When to leave a note vs. when to call the customer
- Photos: what to photograph and why (equipment errors, chemical levels, anything unusual)
- Route efficiency: batching, driving dead zones, communication with dispatch

**SplashLens tie-in:** Weekly Service Checklist + Route Tracker  
**Assessment:** You arrive at a pool: FC 0, pH 8.4, TA 180, CYA 40, algae visible on walls. What's your sequence of actions and why?

---

### MODULE 6: Green Pool Recovery & SLAM (Days 14–17)
**Learning outcome:** Tech can execute a SLAM treatment without hand-holding and explain it to a customer.

Topics:
- What SLAM stands for and why it works (chemical, not mechanical)
- The target FC formula: CYA × 0.4 (green pool), CYA × 0.5 (mustard), CYA × 0.6 (black algae)
- Equipment requirements: pump runtime during SLAM, filter type considerations
- The OCLT (Overnight Chlorine Loss Test): exactly what it is, how to do it, how to pass it
- What happens if you rush it — why cutting SLAM short costs the customer more money
- Customer communication script: "This pool will be ready in [X] days if you do this"

**SplashLens tie-in:** SLAM Calculator + SLAM Multi-Day Tracker  
**Assessment:** Walk through a complete SLAM from test results to OCLT pass (given specific starting numbers)

---

### MODULE 7: Seasonal — Opening & Closing (Days 17–20)
**Learning outcome:** Tech can run an opening or closing without a supervisor.

Topics:
- Opening sequence: de-winterize, inspect, balance, shock, run filter
- Why opening chemistry is different (lower CYA, higher shock dose, 48-hour monitoring)
- Closing sequence: final balance, algaecide, plug, winterize equipment
- Climate-specific differences: freeze protection, mesh vs. solid covers
- What to tell the customer to expect after opening vs. after closing
- Freeze protection: how to check it's working, what to do if a pipe bursts

**SplashLens tie-in:** Opening Checklist + Closing Checklist  
**Assessment:** It's spring. You're opening a pool that was closed last October. FC 0, pH unknown (stain on wall), cover was solid and sitting on water. What's your first 5 steps?

---

### MODULE 8: Robotic Cleaners & Automation (Days 20–22)
**Learning outcome:** Tech can diagnose the most common robotic cleaner issues and explain automation basics to a customer.

Topics:
- How robotic cleaners work: motor, drive tracks, filter bag, brushes
- The 5 most common errors: won't move, runs in circles, leaves debris, tangled cord, waterline stops
- Maytronics/Dolphin specific: cable inspection, filter bag cleaning, error lights
- Pool automation 101: Pentair IntelliTouch/EasyTouch, Hayward OmniLogic, Jandy iAqualink
- When automation is a tech problem vs. an IT problem (pro tip: it's usually the IP address)
- Salt systems: what the tech owns vs. what the homeowner owns

**SplashLens tie-in:** Error Code Database (Maytronics, Polaris, Aiper, Beatbot, Betta sections)

---

### MODULE 9: Customer Communication & Upsell (Days 22–25)
**Learning outcome:** Tech can communicate problems to customers clearly and present options without being pushy.

Topics:
- The "diagnosis, recommendation, cost" script
- How to explain a chemical imbalance to someone who doesn't understand chemistry
- How to present a repair estimate vs. a service upgrade (never lead with the number)
- The 3 things that make customers cancel: lack of communication, surprises on the bill, feeling talked down to
- How to handle an angry customer at the pool
- When to escalate to the owner vs. handle it yourself
- How to present upsells: variable speed pumps, salt systems, robotic cleaners

**Assessment:** Customer calls to say "I came home and the pool is green." Roleplay the call. What do you say in the first 30 seconds?

---

### MODULE 10: Business Foundations for Pool Techs (Days 25–30)
**Learning outcome:** Tech understands how the business works and what makes a route profitable.

Topics:
- How pool service companies make money (route density, chemical margin, repair labor)
- What a "billable hour" is and why it matters for your paycheck
- How to track your route efficiency: stops per day, time per stop, drive time ratio
- Certifications worth getting: CPO (Certified Pool Operator), NSPF, state licenses
- Insurance basics: what the company's liability policy covers vs. what it doesn't
- Career paths: lead tech, route supervisor, service manager, owning your own route

**Bonus content:** How to read a pool service business P&L as a tech  
**Assessment:** Final exam — 30 questions covering all 10 modules. 75% required to pass.
